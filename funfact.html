History of Nintendo Consoles

Extra Fun Fact

Deal With Sony

One of Nintendo’s biggest mistakes was the deal with Sony. Nintendo and Sony planned to create a console together that had a CD-ROM add-on. As the release date came closer, Nintendo got anxious. The deal would allow Sony to have complete control over manufacturing and licensing of CD-based games. They thought Sony would use the console as a way to forced themselves into the gaming industry. Nintendo decided to secretly sign a deal with Sony’s competitor, Philips. At the 1991 Consumer Electronics Show, Sony announced the plans for the console. The next day, Nintendo announced they were working on a CD-based add-on with Philips instead. Sony released the console for themselves in 1995. The console turned out to be the Playstation which sold 102.49 million units.
