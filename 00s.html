History of Nintendo Consoles

Consoles of the 2000's

GameCube (2001)

In 2001, Nintendo created the GameCube. It was their first disc-based system because they mainly used cartridges. It used small optical format to store its games. It also had four controller ports that could link up with the Game Boy Advance.


Nintendo DS (2004)

The DS in 2004 was a game changer. Its revolutionary design consisted of 2 screens with 256 x 192 resolution 3 inch displays. The bottom display was a stylus-base touch screen. Variations of the console were the DSi and DS lite.


Wii (2006)

Wii was a success when it was released in 2006. It had a TV-remote-shaped motion controller. In addition, it came with GameCube controller ports. Games like Wii Sports and Mario Cart became popular among families, kids, and non-gamers.
